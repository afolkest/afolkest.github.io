Where does spacetime tear apart? Is it only at the Big Bang and inside black holes, or is the phenomenon more widespread? This is one of the central questions I worked on during my PhD at MIT and my postdoc at UC Davis. I attacked the question leveraging the deep connection between gravity and quantum information theory.

Before that I've been Fulbright scholar, and I also spent a year at CERN working on particle detectors, before realizing that the screwdriver is better entrusted to someone else.

These days I work on using LLMs to build powertools for scientists at the startup Undermind. We arguably have the most comprehensive scientific literature search engine out there.

Outside work I produce electronic music, make visual arts, and write Mechanics of Aesthetics. I've recently also started tinkering with writing visual arts software and algorithms. I'm currently writing a drawing program using elliptic boundary value problems as the fundamental artistic primitive.
